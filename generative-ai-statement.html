Generative AI Statement

Generative AI has been one of the most prominent topics of conversation ever since it got introduced. There have been many potential uses for the product that have been developed over the past few years such as visual creation, text generation, and operational functionality. But this does require us to make our stance on how AI should be ethically and responsibly used and when it has gone too far, which is what I will be expressing here.

I believed AI can be a tool, it has the potential to move society into a technologically efficient world streamlining simple processes within the business world and personal day-to-day tasks. I was fortunate enough to be a TEDx speaker and talk about the potential of AI when it was first introduced. However my opinion has evolved over time, through recent AI integration and how it has been difficult to identify content of AI from human work.

Human work, such as art and literature, does have a passion and purpose that cannot be replicated by AI, however the product of that work does seem to be very similar to the product of a human. This is where I tend to feel that this must be indicated on pieces of work. When publicly displayed the works must have citation, as visible as possible, in order to not deteriorate any field of art that is existing. I hope that in the future there are rules and regulations about these actions and society adheres to them.

But on the topic of AI's potential, it is unlimited to streamline manual work so society as a whole can progress on focusing their energy and time on something more beneficial. As a woman aspiring to be in tech in the future, I am hopeful that these enhancements will only launch business to more potential to achieve their mission and grow their business to the fullest potential it can without the stress of manual labor efforts or repetitive tasks.
